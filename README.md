# **⌚ InkTime \- Open Source Smartwatch**

This repository contains the hardware design and mechanical assembly (EVT) for **InkTime**, an open-source, energy-efficient smartwatch based on the nRF52840 microcontroller and an E-Paper display. The project was developed with mass production in mind, adhering to strict design and manufacturing standards.

## **🧠 Hardware Functionality Description**

* **Microcontroller (MCU):** The nRF52840 was chosen for its excellent Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) support and minimal power consumption in sleep mode. It handles the internal logic, sensor communication, and display updates. The system is clocked by two external crystals (32MHz for the radio and 32.768kHz for the RTC).  
* **Power Management:** \* Li-Po battery charging is handled via the USB-C port (16-pin) using the BQ25180 IC, which is optimized for wearables.  
  * Battery monitoring is performed by the MAX17048 (Fuel Gauge), which reports the state of charge to the processor via I2C.  
  * A stable 3.3V supply is provided to the system by the RT6160A DC/DC buck-boost regulator.  
* **Sensors & Feedback:**  
  * Motion detection (used for the pedometer and the "Wake on wrist raise" gesture) is handled by the BMA423 IMU. It communicates via I2C and uses an Interrupt pin to wake the processor only when necessary, saving power.  
  * Haptic notifications are generated by a vibration motor intelligently controlled by the DRV2605 driver.  
* **Display:** The E-Paper display ensures massive battery autonomy, drawing power only during image refreshes. It connects via the SPI protocol using a Molex 503480-2400 FPC connector. To save Z-axis space, the battery is soldered directly to dedicated pads on the PCB.

## **🛠️ Design Log & DVT Compliance**

To ensure a seamless and error-free transition to manufacturing, the PCB was routed while strictly adhering to the following constraints and best practices:

* **Mechanical Constraints:** All components were placed exclusively on the TOP layer. The PCB thickness was set to 1mm to ensure proper closure within the enclosure, leaving adequate room for the battery and display.  
* **RF & Antenna (CRITICAL):** The ceramic antenna (2450AT18B100E) was placed at the edge of the board. A complete Keepout zone was implemented underneath it—there are no traces, no copper, and no ground planes on any layer beneath the antenna to prevent BLE signal attenuation.  
* **Routing & Power:** \* Power traces (VCC, VBAT, 3V3, VBUS) were routed with a width of 0.3mm (necking down under BGAs where necessary).  
  * Data signals were routed at 0.15mm.  
  * Vias were avoided on major power traces to prevent voltage drops.  
  * No 90° angles exist on any traces; only 45° angles or curves were utilized.  
* **Decoupling:** 100nF (0201) decoupling capacitors were placed physically as close as possible to the power (VDD) pins of their respective ICs.  
* **Ground Planes (GND):** Ground polygons were poured on both the TOP and BOTTOM layers. Abundant Via Stitching was applied to ensure a short return path and effective electromagnetic shielding, particularly around the RF area and the MCU.  
* **Validation:** The design successfully passes both DRC (using the provided checking profile) and ERC, ignoring permitted errors (e.g., "Only INPUT pins on NET ID" or intentional overlaps at the edge buttons/USB connector). Test pads are clearly exposed and visibly marked on the Silkscreen layer.
